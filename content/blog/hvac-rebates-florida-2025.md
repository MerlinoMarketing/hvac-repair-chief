# Florida HVAC Rebates and Tax Credits for 2025: What Boca Raton Homeowners Qualify For

A new high-efficiency HVAC system is a significant investment—but significant incentives are available to reduce that cost. Federal tax credits, FPL utility rebates, and manufacturer promotions can combine to offset thousands of dollars on qualifying equipment.

Here's a complete breakdown of what's available to Boca Raton and South Florida homeowners in 2025.

## Federal Tax Credit: The 25C Residential Energy Efficient Property Credit

The Inflation Reduction Act dramatically expanded the energy efficiency tax credit (Section 25C) for qualifying home improvements, including HVAC. The current credit is:

**30% of the cost of qualifying HVAC equipment, up to annual limits**

### What HVAC Equipment Qualifies?

| Equipment Type | Maximum Annual Credit | Efficiency Requirement |
|---------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Central AC (split system) | $600 | Must meet highest efficiency tier (ENERGY STAR Most Efficient) |
| Air-source heat pump | $2,000 | Must meet CEE Tier 1 or higher |
| Heat pump water heater | $2,000 | EF 2.0 or higher (most HPWHs qualify) |
| Geothermal heat pump | 30% (no cap) | Must meet Energy Star requirements |
| Biomass boiler | $2,000 | 75%+ efficiency |

**Key details:**
- The $2,000 limit is per YEAR, and can be applied across multiple years
- Must be installed in your PRIMARY residence (not rental properties under current rules)
- Must use a qualified tax preparer to claim (Form 5695)
- You need documentation: the manufacturer's certification statement for the equipment

### Heat Pumps Get the Best Credit

For most South Florida homeowners replacing aging AC systems, a heat pump system (which serves as both heating and cooling) qualifies for the $2,000 annual limit—the highest credit available for HVAC. Combined with 30% of cost:

- $8,000 heat pump system → 30% = $2,400, capped at $2,000 credit
- $12,000 installation → 30% = $3,600, capped at $2,000 credit

The $2,000 credit is a tax credit (not a deduction)—it reduces your actual tax liability dollar for dollar.

## FPL (Florida Power & Light) Rebates

FPL offers rebates for customers who upgrade to qualifying high-efficiency equipment:

### Current FPL HVAC Rebate Amounts (2025)

| Equipment | FPL Rebate |
|-----------|-----------|
| ENERGY STAR certified central AC (SEER2 16+) | $75 |
| High-efficiency central AC (SEER2 18+) | $100–$150 |
| ENERGY STAR certified heat pump | $100 |
| Variable-speed heat pump (SEER2 18+) | $150–$200 |
| Heat pump water heater | $100–$150 |
| Smart thermostat | $50–$75 |

**FPL rebate process:**
1. Purchase and install qualifying equipment through a licensed Florida contractor
2. Submit the rebate application (FPL.com/save) within 90 days of installation
3. Provide equipment documentation (serial number, model number, contractor invoice)
4. Rebate paid by check or bill credit within 6–8 weeks

FPL also offers the On-Bill Finance program for qualifying customers, allowing you to finance the equipment through your electric bill—an excellent option for those who don't want a separate loan.

## Important Limitations and Deadlines

- **Tax credit requires installation in the tax year** — Equipment must be installed (not just purchased) by December 31 to claim that year's credit
- **Manufacturer rebates expire** — Check current offers; they change quarterly
- **FPL rebates require 90-day submission** — Don't miss the window
- **Tax credit is non-refundable** — If your tax liability is less than $2,000, you only receive what you owe; unused credit doesn't roll over (under current law)
- **Consult a tax professional** — Specific eligibility rules and documentation requirements should be verified with your tax advisor

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do Florida rental properties qualify for the 25C HVAC tax credit?**
A: Under current IRS guidelines, the 25C credit applies to your primary residence only. Rental properties do not qualify for 25C.

**Q: Can I claim the credit if I finance the HVAC system?**
A: Yes. The credit is based on the cost of the equipment, not how it's paid for. Whether you pay cash, finance through a contractor, or use a HELOC, the same credit applies.

**Q: Do mini-split systems qualify for rebates and tax credits?**
A: Yes—qualifying mini-split heat pump systems qualify for the $2,000 federal tax credit and FPL rebates, provided they meet the efficiency thresholds. Many high-SEER mini-splits easily exceed the requirements.

**Q: How do I know if the specific system I'm buying qualifies?**
A: Ask your contractor for the manufacturer's certification statement for the equipment. This document confirms 25C eligibility. You can also check the ENERGY STAR product finder at energystar.gov.

**Q: Is there a limit on how many years I can claim the 25C credit?**
A: No annual cap on how many years—but the annual limit applies per tax year. If you replace an AC in 2025 ($2,000 credit) and a water heater in 2026, you can claim up to $2,000 each year.
