# Understanding HVAC Warranties in Florida: What's Covered and What's Not

You just spent $8,000 on a new HVAC system. The contractor hands you a warranty sheet—and it might as well be written in a foreign language. "Limited parts warranty." "Registered vs. unregistered." "Labor not included." What does any of it mean?

Florida homeowners have specific considerations when it comes to HVAC warranties. This guide breaks it all down clearly.

## The Two Types of HVAC Warranty

### 1. Manufacturer's Warranty

The manufacturer's warranty covers defects in materials and workmanship in the equipment itself. This is what the HVAC brand (Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Daikin, etc.) offers.

**What it covers:**
- Compressor failure due to manufacturing defects
- Coil failures from manufacturing issues
- Control board failures
- Any component that fails due to a defect in the part itself

**What it does NOT cover:**
- Normal wear and tear
- Failures caused by improper installation
- Failures caused by lack of maintenance
- Refrigerant leaks from physical damage or corrosion
- Labor costs to diagnose and replace failed parts

### 2. Contractor's Warranty

This covers the installation work itself. A reputable contractor warranties their workmanship for 1–2 years. If a refrigerant line leaks because it was improperly flared during installation, the contractor's warranty should cover both the repair and the refrigerant.

Ask for the workmanship warranty in writing before signing any installation contract.

## Registered vs. Unregistered Warranty

This is where many Florida homeowners get surprised. Most manufacturers offer two warranty tiers:

| Warranty Type | Requirement | Typical Coverage |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Base (unregistered) | None | 5 years parts |
| Extended (registered) | Register within 60–90 days of install | 10 years parts |

**The catch:** You must register the equipment with the manufacturer within the specified window—often 60 or 90 days from installation. Miss that window and you're stuck with the shorter base warranty.

Many contractors handle registration automatically. Ask yours specifically: "Will you register my equipment with the manufacturer?" Get confirmation in writing.

## What Voids Your HVAC Warranty in Florida

Manufacturers can and do deny warranty claims for specific reasons:

### Failure to Maintain

This is the most common warranty denial trigger. Most manufacturer warranties require documentation of regular professional maintenance. If a compressor fails at year 7 and you have no maintenance records, the manufacturer can deny the claim.

**Solution:** Sign up for a maintenance plan with your HVAC company. They maintain service records on your behalf.

### Improper Installation

If equipment was installed by an unlicensed contractor, or if installation errors contributed to a failure, the manufacturer can deny coverage. Always use a licensed Florida HVAC contractor for installation.

### Unauthorized Modifications or Repairs

Having an unlicensed person work on the system, or using non-approved parts, can void the warranty. All service work should be performed by a licensed HVAC technician.

### Mismatched Equipment

Installing a new condenser with an old, non-compatible air handler—or using a different brand's air handler—can void the warranty on both units. Manufacturers test and certify equipment as matched systems.

### Environmental Damage

Flood damage, hurricane damage, and lightning strikes are typically excluded from manufacturer warranty and need to be handled through homeowner's insurance.

## Extended Warranties and Service Contracts

Some homeowners purchase extended warranties from third parties (home warranty companies). Be cautious:

| Concern | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Exclusions | Many have extensive lists of excluded components |
| Pre-existing conditions | Often deny claims for anything deemed "pre-existing" |
| Approved contractors | May require their own contractors who aren't familiar with your system |
| Payouts | Some cap payouts at amounts far below actual repair costs |

If considering a home warranty, read the HVAC sections carefully. HVAC companies' own maintenance plans often provide better value.

## Florida-Specific Warranty Considerations

Florida law (Florida Statutes Chapter 634) regulates service warranty contracts. Key points:

- Service warranty companies must be licensed in Florida
- Contracts must clearly disclose what's covered and excluded
- Consumers have cancellation rights

The Florida Department of Financial Services oversees warranty companies—you can verify registration at their website.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does my HVAC warranty transfer if I sell my home?**
A: Some warranties transfer to subsequent owners; others are non-transferable. Carrier, Lennox, and Trane all have different transfer policies. Check your specific documentation, or call the manufacturer. A transferable warranty can be a selling point.

**Q: My AC failed—how do I make a warranty claim?**
A: Contact your installing contractor first. They will diagnose the failure, determine if it's a warranty-covered defect, and coordinate the parts claim with the manufacturer. The warranty covers parts; you'll likely still pay labor.

**Q: What if my contractor is out of business?**
A: You can take your unit to any authorized dealer/service provider for the manufacturer. The parts warranty follows the equipment, not the installer.

**Q: How long is a typical labor warranty from a Florida HVAC contractor?**
A: Most reputable contractors offer 1–2 years on workmanship. Some premium installation packages include longer labor warranties (3–5 years). This varies significantly—always ask before choosing a contractor.
