# Do You Need a Permit for HVAC Work in Boca Raton? Florida Rules Explained

"Do I need a permit for that?" is one of the most common questions Boca Raton homeowners ask before HVAC work. The honest answer: usually yes, but the specifics depend on what's being done.

Understanding Florida's permitting requirements protects you from unlicensed work, voided warranties, insurance complications, and problems when you sell your home.

## Why HVAC Permits Matter

Permits aren't just bureaucratic red tape. They serve real protective functions:

- **Safety inspection** — A licensed inspector verifies the work meets Florida Building Code
- **Insurance protection** — Unpermitted work can invalidate homeowner's insurance claims related to that system
- **Warranty validity** — Manufacturer warranties often require permitted, licensed installation
- **Home sale protection** — Unpermitted work discovered during a home sale can derail transactions or require costly remediation

## Florida HVAC Work That Requires a Permit

Florida Statute 489 and the Florida Building Code govern HVAC work. Generally, a permit is required for:

- **Full system replacement** (air handler + heat pump/condenser) — Always requires permit in Florida
- **Air handler replacement only** — Permit required
- **Condenser/heat pump replacement only** — Permit required
- **New equipment installation** (mini-splits, packaged units) — Permit required
- **Duct replacement or major modification** — Permit required
- **Refrigerant system changes** — When accompanied by equipment replacement

Boca Raton operates under Palm Beach County building codes, which follow and sometimes exceed Florida Building Code requirements.

## HVAC Work That Does NOT Require a Permit

Routine maintenance and minor repairs typically don't require permits:

- Air filter replacement
- Thermostat replacement (like for like, no system changes)
- Minor refrigerant additions (recharge only, no system changes)
- Condenser coil cleaning
- Drain line clearing
- Capacitor or contactor replacement
- Fan motor replacement

The line between repair and replacement isn't always obvious. When in doubt, ask your licensed contractor.

## Who Can Pull an HVAC Permit in Florida?

In Florida, HVAC permits must be pulled by a licensed contractor—specifically, a contractor holding:

- **State Certified HVAC Contractor license (CAC)**, or
- **State Registered HVAC Contractor license (valid for specific county)**

Homeowners can technically pull owner-builder permits for their primary residence, but the work must still pass inspection and you assume full responsibility for compliance.

**Never hire an unlicensed contractor** for permitted work. If something goes wrong, you have no legal recourse, and you may be liable for all remediation costs.

## The Permit Process in Boca Raton

For most HVAC replacements, the process looks like this:

1. **Contractor submits permit application** to the City of Boca Raton Building Department (or Palm Beach County for unincorporated areas)
2. **Permit issued** — often same-day or next-day for standard residential HVAC
3. **Work performed**
4. **Inspection scheduled** — an inspector visits to verify installation
5. **Permit closed** — becomes part of your home's public record

Most licensed HVAC companies handle all permitting as part of the installation. Ask explicitly: "Will you pull the permit, and is that included in your price?"

## Red Flags: Contractors Who Skip Permits

A contractor who offers to "skip the permit to save you money" is a red flag. This practice:

- Exposes you to fines if discovered ($500–$5,000+ in Florida)
- Creates title and insurance issues
- May indicate the contractor is unlicensed
- Leaves you with no recourse if work is done incorrectly

Legitimate, licensed HVAC contractors pull permits as a matter of course. It's part of professional practice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What happens if HVAC work was done without a permit in my home?**
A: You may need to apply for an after-the-fact permit, have the work inspected (which may require opening walls), or demolish and redo non-compliant work. This is much more expensive than pulling the permit initially. Disclose this to buyers during home sales.

**Q: How long is an HVAC permit valid in Boca Raton?**
A: Florida building permits are typically valid for 180 days from issuance, with provisions to extend. Work should begin within the permit period.

**Q: Does my HVAC contractor need a specific license in Boca Raton?**
A: Yes. Verify your contractor holds a Florida CAC (Certified Air-Conditioning Contractor) or equivalent license. You can verify licenses at myfloridalicense.com.

**Q: Can I sell my home if HVAC work was done without a permit?**
A: You can try, but buyers, inspectors, and lenders often discover unpermitted work. In Florida, sellers must disclose known unpermitted work. Resolving it before listing is strongly recommended.
