# Signs Your Ductwork Needs Inspection or Replacement in South Florida

Most Boca Raton homeowners never think about their ductwork—until something goes wrong. That's a problem, because duct issues silently drain energy efficiency, compromise indoor air quality, and stress your HVAC system without triggering any obvious alarms.

The EPA estimates that 20–30% of the air moving through a typical duct system is lost to leaks, holes, and poor connections. In South Florida, where your attic runs 130–150°F in summer, those leaks are pulling scalding, humid air directly into your conditioned spaces.

## Common Warning Signs of Duct Problems

### 1. Uneven Temperatures Room to Room

If some rooms are consistently harder to cool than others despite a properly functioning AC unit, duct leaks or obstructions are a likely cause. Air meant for a far bedroom may be escaping into the attic long before it arrives.

### 2. Suddenly Higher Electric Bills

A significant jump in your FPL bill without a corresponding change in usage habits often traces back to duct leaks. Your system is working harder to push the same amount of conditioned air to your living spaces.

### 3. Musty or Stale Smells from Vents

South Florida attics accumulate mold, dust, insect debris, and other contaminants. When duct leaks allow attic air to enter the system, these odors circulate through your home. This is also a significant indoor air quality concern.

### 4. Excessive Dust on Registers and Furniture

Return leaks—on the return side of the duct system—pull unconditioned attic air and its contents through your system. If you notice dust accumulating quickly near vents, suspect return duct leaks.

### 5. AC Running Constantly Without Reaching Setpoint

When conditioned air leaks before reaching its destination, your system can run indefinitely without satisfying the thermostat. This indicates significant supply duct leakage.

### 6. Visible Damage on Accessible Ducts

If you can access portions of your duct system (in a crawl space, mechanical room, or through an attic hatch), look for:

- Disconnected flex duct sections
- Collapsed or kinked flex duct
- Damaged foil-faced insulation
- Tape peeling at connections

## Types of Ductwork Used in South Florida Homes

| Type | Common in FL? | Lifespan | Issues |
|------|---------------|----------|--------|
| Flexible duct (flex duct) | Very common | 15–25 years | Prone to kinking, rodent damage |
| Sheet metal | Older homes | 30–50+ years | Sags, joint separation, rust |
| Duct board (fiberglass) | Moderate | 15–20 years | Delaminates in humidity |

Flex duct is the most common in South Florida construction and the most susceptible to humidity damage and rodent intrusion. Attic flex duct runs degrade faster here than in other climates.

## Professional Duct Testing Methods

If you suspect duct problems, a professional can conduct:

**Duct blaster test** — Pressurizes the duct system and measures leakage. Results are reported as CFM25 leakage; Florida Energy Code sets acceptable leakage limits.

**Blower door test** — Tests whole-house air tightness and can help identify if duct leaks are contributing to building envelope issues.

**Thermal imaging** — An infrared camera identifies temperature anomalies caused by duct leaks, especially in attic spaces.

## When to Repair vs. Replace Ductwork

**Repair is appropriate when:**
- Leaks are isolated and accessible
- The duct system is less than 15 years old
- Damage is caused by a specific event (pest intrusion, installation error)

**Replacement is appropriate when:**
- The system is 20+ years old with widespread deterioration
- Flex duct has collapsed or degraded throughout
- Multiple previous repairs haven't solved the problem
- You're installing a new HVAC system (always pair new equipment with inspected ducts)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long do ducts last in South Florida?**
A: Flex duct in South Florida attics typically lasts 15–25 years. The combination of heat cycling, humidity, and occasional pest pressure accelerates degradation compared to northern climates.

**Q: Can duct leaks cause health problems?**
A: Yes. Return duct leaks can pull mold spores, fiberglass particles, rodent droppings, and pest dander from the attic into your living spaces. This is a real indoor air quality concern, especially for allergy and asthma sufferers.

**Q: Is duct sealing worth it?**
A: For most South Florida homes with aging ductwork, yes. Professional duct sealing (with mastic sealant or Aeroseal pressurized injection) can recover 15–30% of lost efficiency and improve comfort significantly.

**Q: Do I need permits to replace ductwork in Florida?**
A: In most cases, yes. A licensed HVAC contractor handles permitting as part of the project. Work without permits can cause issues during home sales.
