# Emergency HVAC FAQs -- Boca Raton, FL

## What qualifies as an HVAC emergency?

Any situation where the loss of cooling creates a health risk or risk of property damage is an HVAC emergency. This includes complete cooling failure during extreme heat, AC failure with elderly or medically vulnerable occupants present, burning smells from the system, and condensate overflow causing water damage.

## How fast can you respond to an emergency in Boca Raton?

We aim to respond to emergency calls in Boca Raton and nearby areas within one to three hours depending on time of day and current demand. During extreme heat events when call volume is high, we prioritize based on health risk and communicate estimated arrival times clearly.

## Is emergency service more expensive than regular service?

Emergency and after-hours service typically carries a higher dispatch fee than standard scheduled service. We are transparent about this when you call. For most customers in a genuine emergency, the fee is worth the rapid response.

## What should I do while waiting for the technician?

Close blinds and curtains to reduce solar heat gain. Move vulnerable family members to the coolest room in the house. Use fans to improve air circulation. Consider a portable cooler or window unit as temporary relief if you have one. If anyone shows signs of heat illness, seek medical attention immediately.

## Can you fix it in one visit?

The vast majority of emergency AC failures are resolved in a single visit. Our technicians carry the most commonly needed parts. Rare scenarios involving unavailable parts will require a follow-up, but we will communicate clearly and restore partial cooling if possible.

## What if my system cannot be repaired quickly?

If your system needs a part we do not have in stock, we will give you an honest timeline and recommend temporary measures. In cases where the system is beyond economic repair, we can arrange expedited replacement.

Yes. We cover all Boca Raton zip codes plus Delray Beach, Boynton Beach, Deerfield Beach, Pompano Beach, and surrounding South Florida communities for emergency service.
