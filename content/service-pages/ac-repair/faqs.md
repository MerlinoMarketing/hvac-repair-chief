# AC Repair FAQs — Boca Raton, FL

## How do I know if my AC needs repair or replacement?

If your system is under 10 years old and the repair cost is less than half the cost of a new unit, repair usually makes sense. If the compressor has failed on a system older than 12 years, replacement is often the better investment. Our technicians provide honest recommendations — we will never push you toward a replacement you do not need.

## How long does an AC repair take?

Most repairs in Boca Raton homes are completed within two to three hours. Capacitor replacements, refrigerant recharges, and drain cleanings are typically done in under an hour. Compressor replacements or major coil repairs may take longer and could require a return visit if a part needs to be ordered.

## Why is my AC blowing warm air?

Warm air is most commonly caused by low refrigerant (often from a leak), a failed compressor, a tripped breaker on the outdoor unit, or a dirty condenser coil. Our technician will diagnose the exact cause during the service visit.

## How often should AC refrigerant be recharged?

A properly functioning AC system does not consume refrigerant — it is a closed loop. If your system is low on refrigerant, there is a leak that must be found and repaired. Simply adding refrigerant without fixing the leak is a temporary band-aid.

## How can I prevent future AC breakdowns?

The best prevention is a routine maintenance plan. Changing filters regularly, keeping the outdoor unit clear of debris, and scheduling annual tune-ups dramatically reduce the chance of unexpected breakdowns — especially important heading into South Florida summer.
