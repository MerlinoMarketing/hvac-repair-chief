# Heating Repair FAQs -- Boca Raton, FL

## Why is my heat pump blowing cold air in heating mode?

The most common causes are a failed reversing valve, low refrigerant, or the outdoor unit being in a normal defrost cycle. Defrost cycles are brief -- the system should return to heating mode within a few minutes. If cold air continues for more than 15 minutes, call for service.

## Do I need a furnace in Boca Raton?

Almost no homes in Boca Raton use gas furnaces. The standard heating system is a heat pump, which moves heat rather than generating it. Heat pumps are ideal for South Florida mild winters because they are highly efficient at moderate outdoor temperatures.

## How often should my heating system be serviced?

We recommend annual maintenance before the cool season -- typically in October or November. This ensures everything is working before you actually need heat.

## My heat pump is making a loud noise when heating -- what is wrong?

Unusual noises during heating operation can indicate a failing reversing valve, a refrigerant issue, or a mechanical problem with the compressor or fan. Do not ignore unusual sounds -- they typically indicate a problem that will worsen over time.

## Is it worth repairing an older heat pump?

If the system is less than 10 years old and the repair cost is reasonable, repair is usually the right choice. Older systems with repeated failures or a failed compressor may be better candidates for replacement. We give honest recommendations based on your specific situation.

## What if my heating strips are not working?

Electric heating strips in the air handler serve as supplemental or emergency heat. A failed strip heater, blown fuse, or tripped breaker can disable this backup heat source. Our technicians can diagnose and repair electric strip heat as part of a heating service call.
