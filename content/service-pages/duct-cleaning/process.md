# Our Duct Cleaning Process in Boca Raton

HVAC Repair Chief follows a systematic, thorough duct cleaning process that meets NADCA (National Air Duct Cleaners Association) standards. Here is what you can expect.

## Step 1: Pre-Cleaning Inspection

Before we begin, we inspect your duct system using cameras and visual checks. We document the condition of the ducts, identify any areas of concern such as mold growth or pest intrusion, and note any damaged sections that should be repaired.

## Step 2: System Protection

We protect your home by covering furniture and flooring near work areas. All supply and return vents are prepared for the cleaning process, and the HVAC system is shut down safely.

## Step 3: Negative Pressure Setup

We connect commercial-grade vacuum equipment to the main trunk line, creating negative pressure throughout the entire duct system. This means that as we dislodge debris, it moves toward our equipment rather than back into your home.

## Step 4: Agitation and Debris Removal

Working from the farthest registers back toward the main trunk, we use rotary brushes, air whips, and compressed air tools to dislodge accumulated debris from duct walls. The negative pressure system captures it all.

## Step 5: Air Handler and Coil Inspection

We inspect the air handler cabinet, blower wheel, and evaporator coil. If these components have significant buildup, we can address them as part of the service. A dirty evaporator coil undermines the benefit of clean ducts.

## Step 6: Vent Cover Cleaning

All supply and return vent covers are removed, cleaned, and reinstalled. Dirty vent covers reintroduce debris into the airstream.

## Step 7: Post-Cleaning Inspection

We conduct a final inspection, including camera verification of key duct sections. We photograph the results and share them with you.

## Step 8: System Restart and Verification

We restart the HVAC system and verify that airflow is normal throughout all zones. We confirm there are no air leaks introduced during the cleaning process.
